Use Case 03 · Pharmaceutical Integrity

Nexivarel Formulary Integrity Review

Fourteen major adverse cardiovascular events following formulary approval prompted a complete causal audit — tracing a six-hop chain from manufacturer submission to patient harm, and sealing the evidence for FDA MedWatch submission.

Primitives8 MCP tools Causal chain6 hops Timeline steps12 OutputFDA MedWatch filing

Clinical Background

Nexivarel — a novel anticoagulant targeting Factor XIa — entered the hospital formulary at Aldermoor Medical Center following a unanimous P&T committee vote in February 2025. The approval was based on a manufacturer-submitted safety data package citing a pivotal Phase III trial with a favorable MACE rate versus the standard of care. Formulary entry triggered prescribing across three inpatient cardiology services within six weeks.

By May 2025, the adverse event monitoring system had flagged fourteen MACE events — including three non-fatal MIs, four strokes, and seven cases of major bleeding — in patients receiving Nexivarel. The rate far exceeded the Phase III reported incidence. A formulary integrity review was initiated to determine whether the causal chain from manufacturer submission to adverse events was traceable, and whether the approval process itself had been compromised.

Node Inventory

The investigation ingested eleven distinct node categories. Each was annotated via aion_annotate and signed. The manufacturer's submission package and the P&T committee voting record — both existing documents in the hospital's document management system — were ingested as content-addressed capsules. Their BLAKE3 digests now serve as tamper-evident identifiers for the submission and the vote.

Critical to the investigation's integrity: the two committee member conflict nodes were annotated with their disclosed relationships before the policy enforcement step. Their presence in the node corpus — with their full relationship data — is part of the immutable record, even though they were subsequently excluded from the analytical context.

Step 1 · Causal Chain: aion_path

The central question of the integrity review was whether a direct, traceable causal chain existed from the manufacturer's formulary submission to the adverse events. A broken or ambiguous chain would limit regulatory options. A complete chain would establish the grounds for a full MedWatch filing. aion_path was called with a causal constraint over the full node set.

Step 01 · aion_path — Submission to 14 MACE Events
aion_path

→ [causal] → pt-committee-vote → [causal] → formulary-entry
→ [causal] → prescribing-data → [causal] → 14 × MACE-event

6 hops. Complete. The causal chain from manufacturer submission to patient harm is unbroken and graph-traversable. Every edge is typed, every node is content-addressed, every link is signed. The chain does not depend on expert interpretation — it is read directly from the graph structure.

Step 2 · Impact Analysis: aion_impact

From the formulary-entry node — the formal act of approving Nexivarel for prescribing — what was the full forward impact? How many downstream nodes were reachable, and at what depth? This pass was used to establish the scope of the review for regulatory purposes.

Every MACE event is reachable from the formulary entry in two hops. The regulatory filing is reachable in three. The impact analysis establishes that the formulary decision — a single institutional act — created a causal exposure spanning all fourteen adverse events.

Step 3 · Approval Timeline: aion_journal_append

The investigation required a complete chronological record of the approval process — not a narrative summary, but an ordered, content-addressed sequence of institutional acts, each linked to the document or decision node it references. aion_journal_append constructed a 12-step episode from the initial submission receipt to the final adverse event report.

Step 4 · Policy Enforcement: Conflict of Interest Exclusion

During the journal construction, investigation of the committee member records revealed that two members had undisclosed financial relationships with Harfield Pharmaceuticals: one through a speaking engagement honorarium received 90 days before the vote, and one through a spouse's equity holding in the manufacturer's parent company. Neither relationship was disclosed on the conflict of interest form at Step 04 of the timeline.

Both nodes were excluded from the analytical context via aion_deny. Critically, exclusion from the analytical context is not deletion. Both committee member conflict nodes remain in the full node corpus with their complete relationship data, signed and content-addressed. The denial is itself recorded in the snapshot. The regulatory record contains both the evidence of the conflict and the evidence that the analysis was conducted without it.

The analytical passes — path, impact, journal — were re-run on the 9-node context (excluding the two conflict members) to confirm that the causal chain was not an artifact of their participation in the graph construction. It was not. The 6-hop chain from submission to MACE events is fully intact in the excluded-member context.

Step 5 · Milestones: aion_milestones

The regulatory submission required explicit identification of decision checkpoints within the 12-step timeline — moments where an institutional act created a material change in the causal chain. aion_milestones was called to flag pt-committee-vote and fda-medwatch-filing as milestone nodes. These are the two acts with the highest regulatory significance: the vote that authorized prescribing, and the filing that acknowledges the resulting harm.

The committee vote is the institutional act that converted a manufacturer request into a formulary authorization. The MedWatch filing is the regulatory response that the 14 MACE events made obligatory. Both are signed and content-addressed; their timestamps are part of the sealed record. Both are now queryable as milestone nodes — surfaced first in any milestone-filtered graph traversal.

Step 6 · Evidence Seal: aion_snapshot

With the causal chain established, the timeline complete, conflicts excluded, and milestones flagged, the full investigation context was sealed. The snapshot covers every node, every typed edge, the 12-step journal, the milestone flags, and the two denial records. The BLAKE3 digest and Ed25519 signature are attached to the FDA MedWatch filing as proof of the integrity review's completeness and non-repudiability.

For regulatory purposes, the sealed snapshot means: every claim in the MedWatch filing is backed by a content-addressed, cryptographically signed graph. Any future attempt to alter a document in the chain — the safety data package, the committee vote record, a MACE event report — will produce a hash mismatch against the sealed snapshot. Tampering is structurally detectable without relying on a trusted custodian.

Key Findings

  • 01 6-step causal chain traced: manufacturer submission to 14 MACE events. The path from Harfield's formulary request to patient harm is unbroken, graph-traversable, and requires no expert interpretation. Every hop is a typed, signed causal edge.
  • 02 12-step approval timeline: complete record from submission to adverse events. The journal provides an ordered, content-addressed chronicle of the institutional approval process, from the initial submission receipt through the regulatory response. Every step is linked to its corresponding node.
  • 03 Conflict of interest: two committee members denied for undisclosed manufacturer ties. One member failed to disclose a speaking honorarium within the 12-month lookback period. One member failed to disclose a spouse's equity holding. Both undisclosed relationships meet the materiality threshold under 21 CFR Part 54. Both denial records are part of the sealed evidence.
  • 04 Tamper-evident seal for FDA regulatory submission. The BLAKE3 + Ed25519 snapshot provides a mathematically verifiable record of the investigation's completeness. Post-seal document modification is structurally detectable without reliance on a trusted custodian.
  • 05 Complete chain of custody: every node signed, every edge typed, every step verifiable. The investigation produces not just a report but a verifiable artifact. Any claim in the FDA submission can be traced back to a specific content-addressed node, signed by the annotating officer, linked by a typed edge to every downstream consequence.
