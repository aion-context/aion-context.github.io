Use Case 04 · AML Investigation

Operation Shell Game

How AION's graph primitives traced a multi-layered money laundering network through twelve interconnected entities — and sealed the evidence for regulatory submission.

Primitives8 MCP tools Nodes12 Analytics6 passes OutputSAR filing + sealed evidence

Background

Financial intelligence unit analysts received an alert: a series of structurally identical wire transfers, routed through correspondent banking channels, shared timing characteristics consistent with layering — the second phase of classic money laundering. The originating entities were opaque shell companies registered across three jurisdictions. No single data source made the pattern visible. The pattern only emerged by treating the full entity universe as a graph.

The investigation ingested twelve nodes into AION: four shell holding companies, two trading entities, two KYC filings (one contradictory), a transaction cluster, a correspondent bank, a SAR filing node, and a sanction alert. Viktor Sorokin, the beneficial owner of record on one filing, and Elena Marchetti, listed as a signatory but subject to an active access restriction, were also annotated into the substrate.

Policy Enforcement: Access Restriction

Before any analytical pass, the policy layer was invoked. Elena Marchetti — listed as a named signatory on kyc-filing-B — was subject to an active regulatory access restriction. aion_deny was called with her node identifier. From that point forward, every query, path computation, and recall pass operated on an 11-node context. The denial is cryptographically recorded: it appears in the subsequent snapshot digest, making the restriction tamper-evident and auditable.

Path Analysis: Owner to Bank

The first analytical question was direct: what is the shortest causal path from Viktor Sorokin, the beneficial owner, to the correspondent bank through which the transfers cleared? aion_path traversed the graph with a causal edge constraint.

Impact Analysis: Blast Radius of Phantom Holdings

With the causal chain established, the investigation needed to quantify reach. If Phantom Holdings were the instrument of placement — the first phase of laundering — what was the full set of nodes it could contaminate in a forward traversal up to six hops? aion_impact returned a ranked list by depth.

Contradiction Detection: The KYC Cycle

Two KYC filings existed for the same beneficial ownership structure. kyc-filing-A claimed temporal precedence over kyc-filing-B. kyc-filing-B claimed to supersede and replace kyc-filing-A. aion_contradictions detected the impossible cycle immediately.

Step 04 · aion_contradictions — KYC Temporal Conflict
aion_contradictions
kyc-filing-A ↔ kyc-filing-B

Finding: Temporal cycle confirmed. kyc-filing-A asserts it precedes kyc-filing-B and is therefore the authoritative record. kyc-filing-B asserts it supersedes and replaces kyc-filing-A. Both claims cannot simultaneously be true — the graph encodes an impossible temporal ordering.

This contradiction is characteristic of deliberate document layering: two filings with contradictory provenance claims create regulatory ambiguity about which record governs. The substrate surfaced this automatically without manual document comparison.

Graph health at seal time: 7% sparse — consistent with deliberate compartmentalization

Key Findings

  • 01 Three-hop causal chain confirmed. Sorokin → Phantom Holdings → txn-cluster → correspondent-bank. The chain of control from beneficial owner to settlement institution is direct and graph-traversable.
  • 02 Full network is a single community. All 12 entities belong to one cohesive cluster. No node is incidental — every entity plays a structural role in the ring.
  • 03 KYC filings encode an impossible temporal cycle. The contradiction between kyc-filing-A and kyc-filing-B is not an administrative error — it is a deliberate strategy to create competing authoritative records. Both are admissible as evidence of intent.
  • 04 Dream pass extends Sorokin's reach to all four shells. The latent graph — derived from spreading activation alone — reveals that Sorokin's structural influence extends to every entity in the cluster, including those for which no direct documentation exists.
  • 05 Elena Marchetti excluded under policy. Her denial is encoded in the snapshot and is non-repudiable. Eleven of twelve nodes form the evidentiary context for the SAR filing.

Evidence Seal

Following all six analytical passes, the investigation context was sealed with aion_snapshot. The snapshot captures the full node and edge state — 102 nodes and 598 edges across the broader investigation context — as a BLAKE3-chained, Ed25519-signed digest. This seal is submitted alongside the SAR filing as a tamper-evident chain of custody.
